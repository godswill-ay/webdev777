About SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being

SDG 3 is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by the United Nations in 2015. The aim of SDG 3 is to ensure health and well-being for all, at every stage of life. This goal is critical to building prosperous societies as physical and mental health are the foundation of human capability.

This page and our project are dedicated to raising awareness, sharing knowledge, and encouraging action towards achieving SDG 3's targets. Through our focus on various aspects such as health systems, sustainable health behaviors, supportive policies, and community health initiatives, we contribute to the global effort of promoting health and well-being.

SDG 3: Health and Well-being Goals

The Sustainable Development Goal 3 aims to ensure health and well-being for all, at every stage of life. The following are the key targets set to achieve this goal:

  • Reduce maternal mortality and end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age.
  • Fight epidemics like AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and neglected tropical diseases, and combat hepatitis, water-borne diseases, and other communicable diseases.
  • Achieve universal health coverage, including access to essential medicines and vaccines.
  • Reduce deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals, air, water, and soil pollution and contamination.
